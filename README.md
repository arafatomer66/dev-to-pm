## 💡 Why this exists

Developers have a real edge when moving into product management — you understand feasibility, technical debt, system design, and how software actually gets built. What's usually missing is the *other half*: business strategy, go-to-market, pricing, stakeholder influence, and the discipline of working on the right problem before writing a single line of code.

This knowledge base covers that half — **written specifically for a developer audience**, with honest takes, failure modes, and analogies to the engineering world you come from. No framework worship, no certification sales pitch.

The Product Manager role is broader than the Product Owner role. A PM owns the *why* (strategy), the *what* (discovery and prioritization), and coordinates the *how it gets to market* (launch, pricing, adoption). If you want the delivery-focused, Scrum-specific path, see [dev-to-po](https://github.com/arafatomer66/dev-to-po). This repo is for the full product role.
