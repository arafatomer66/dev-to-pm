# Glossary

Every product management term used in this knowledge base, A–Z. Includes cross-references to the page where each term is covered in depth.

---

**A/B Test** — A controlled experiment where two versions (A and B) are shown to random user subsets to measure which performs better. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**Acceptance Criteria** — The specific conditions that must be met for a user story to be considered complete. → [Writing PRDs](02-core-skills/writing-prds.md)

**AARRR (Pirate Metrics)** — A framework mapping metrics to funnel stages: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral. → [Metrics & Analytics](04-product-discovery/metrics-and-analytics.md)

**ACV (Annual Contract Value)** — The average annualized revenue per customer contract. Related to ARR.

**Affinity Mapping** — A synthesis technique for grouping related observations from research into themes. → [User Research](04-product-discovery/user-research.md)

**APM (Associate Product Manager)** — Entry-level PM program at larger tech companies. → [Certifications & Resources](08-career/certifications-and-resources.md)

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** — The annualized value of all active subscription contracts. → [Finance & P&L](07-business-skills/finance-and-pl.md)

**Assumption Testing** — The practice of identifying and testing the beliefs that underlie a product decision before building. → [Discovery & Validation](04-product-discovery/discovery-and-validation.md)

**BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)** — Your walkaway option if a negotiation fails. → [Negotiation](02-core-skills/negotiation.md)

**Backlog** — An ordered list of work to be done, managed by the PM or PO. Related concept in dev-to-po.

**BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)** — A writing style where the conclusion leads, followed by supporting detail. → [Communication & Storytelling](02-core-skills/communication-and-storytelling.md)

**Blue Ocean Strategy** — A framework for creating uncontested market space rather than competing in existing markets. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**Build Trap** — The organizational tendency to measure success by output (features shipped) rather than outcome (value delivered). → [Discovery & Validation](04-product-discovery/discovery-and-validation.md)

**Business Case** — A financial and strategic argument for investing in a product initiative. → [Finance & P&L](07-business-skills/finance-and-pl.md)

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — Total sales and marketing spend divided by new customers acquired. → [Unit Economics](07-business-skills/unit-economics.md)

**Champion** — An internal advocate at a customer account who lobbied for your product and has personal credibility invested in its success. → [B2B Products](12-special-contexts/b2b-products.md)

**Churn** — Customers or revenue lost in a period. → [Business Models](07-business-skills/business-models.md)

**Cohort Analysis** — Tracking a group of users (cohort) over time to understand behavior and retention. → [Metrics & Analytics](04-product-discovery/metrics-and-analytics.md)

**Competitive Intelligence** — The practice of understanding competitors' products, strategies, and market positions. → [Competitive Intelligence](03-product-strategy/competitive-intelligence.md)

**Concierge MVP** — A manually-delivered service that validates value before building the automated product. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**Configuration vs. Customization** — Configuration = product-native settings; Customization = engineering work per customer. → [B2B Products](12-special-contexts/b2b-products.md)

**Continuous Discovery** — The practice of maintaining a weekly cadence of customer contact throughout product development. → [Discovery & Validation](04-product-discovery/discovery-and-validation.md)

**Counter-Metric** — A guardrail metric that prevents gaming of the primary metric. → [OKRs & Metrics](03-product-strategy/okrs-and-metrics.md)

**Design Partner** — A customer who commits to testing an early-stage feature in exchange for input into its development. → [B2B Products](12-special-contexts/b2b-products.md)

**Developer Experience (DX)** — The quality of the developer's experience building on a platform or API. → [Platform Products](12-special-contexts/platform-products.md)

**Diagnosis** — In Rumelt's strategy framework, the specific challenge or opportunity a strategy is addressing. → [Vision & Strategy](03-product-strategy/vision-and-strategy.md)

**Discovery** — The practice of figuring out what to build before building it. → [Discovery & Validation](04-product-discovery/discovery-and-validation.md)

**ERRC (Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create)** — The Blue Ocean strategy action framework. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**Ecosystem** — The network of partners, developers, and integrations built on top of a platform. → [Platform Products](12-special-contexts/platform-products.md)

**Feature Flag** — A mechanism to enable or disable features for specific users without a code deploy. → [Launch Planning](05-go-to-market/launch-planning.md)

**Freemium** — A pricing model where a core product is free and advanced capabilities require payment. → [Pricing & Packaging](05-go-to-market/pricing-and-packaging.md)

**Gross Margin** — Revenue minus cost of goods sold, expressed as a percentage of revenue. → [Finance & P&L](07-business-skills/finance-and-pl.md)

**HIPAA** — US regulation governing protected health information. → [Regulated Environments](12-special-contexts/regulated-environments.md)

**HiPPO (Highest Paid Person's Opinion)** — The tendency for the most senior person's preference to override data-informed judgment. → [Stakeholder Management](02-core-skills/stakeholder-management.md)

**How Might We (HMW)** — A workshop technique for reframing problems as opportunities. → [Facilitation & Workshops](02-core-skills/facilitation-and-workshops.md)

**Hypothesis** — A falsifiable prediction about what will happen if a product decision is made. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**ICE Scoring** — A prioritization framework: Impact × Confidence × Ease. → [Prioritization Frameworks](02-core-skills/prioritization-frameworks.md)

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — A detailed description of the company (in B2B) that would benefit most from your product. → [Market Analysis](03-product-strategy/market-analysis.md)

**Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)** — A framework for understanding the underlying motivation behind a product usage or purchase. → [Personas & JTBD](04-product-discovery/personas-and-jtbd.md)

**Kano Model** — A framework for classifying features into: Basic, Performance, Excitement (delighters), Indifferent, and Reverse. → [Prioritization Frameworks](02-core-skills/prioritization-frameworks.md)

**Key Results (KRs)** — The measurable outcomes that indicate progress toward an OKR Objective. → [OKRs & Metrics](03-product-strategy/okrs-and-metrics.md)

**Lagging Indicator** — A metric that measures what has already happened. → [Data-Driven Decisions](02-core-skills/data-driven-decisions.md)

**Launch Tier** — A classification of launch size that determines the level of go-to-market investment. → [Launch Planning](05-go-to-market/launch-planning.md)

**Leading Indicator** — A metric that predicts future outcomes. → [Data-Driven Decisions](02-core-skills/data-driven-decisions.md)

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — The total revenue expected from a customer over their relationship with you. → [Unit Economics](07-business-skills/unit-economics.md)

**Market Development** — Ansoff Matrix growth strategy: existing product, new markets. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**Market Penetration** — Ansoff Matrix growth strategy: existing product, existing markets. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**Messaging Brief** — A document that aligns product and marketing on how a feature or product will be communicated. → [Working with Marketing](05-go-to-market/working-with-marketing.md)

**Minimum Detectable Effect (MDE)** — The smallest change a test is designed to detect. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**MoSCoW** — Prioritization framework: Must have / Should have / Could have / Won't have. → [Prioritization Frameworks](02-core-skills/prioritization-frameworks.md)

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** — Monthly pulse of subscription revenue. → [Finance & P&L](07-business-skills/finance-and-pl.md)

**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** — The minimum experiment needed to test the most important assumption. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — A customer satisfaction metric based on likelihood to recommend. → [Metrics & Analytics](04-product-discovery/metrics-and-analytics.md)

**NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** — The percentage of last period's revenue retained, plus expansion, minus churn. → [Unit Economics](07-business-skills/unit-economics.md)

**North Star Metric** — The single metric that best captures whether the product is delivering value. → [OKRs & Metrics](03-product-strategy/okrs-and-metrics.md)

**Objective** — In OKRs, the qualitative, inspirational goal for a period. → [OKRs & Metrics](03-product-strategy/okrs-and-metrics.md)

**OKR (Objective and Key Result)** — A goal-setting framework: qualitative Objective + measurable Key Results. → [OKRs & Metrics](03-product-strategy/okrs-and-metrics.md)

**Opportunity Score** — A metric combining importance and satisfaction to identify underserved customer needs. → [Prioritization Frameworks](02-core-skills/prioritization-frameworks.md)

**Opportunity-Solution Tree** — A visual structure linking desired outcomes → opportunities → solutions → experiments. → [Discovery & Validation](04-product-discovery/discovery-and-validation.md)

**P&L (Profit & Loss)** — Financial statement tracking revenue and costs. → [Finance & P&L](07-business-skills/finance-and-pl.md)

**Payback Period** — Time to recover the cost of acquiring a customer from their gross profit contribution. → [Unit Economics](07-business-skills/unit-economics.md)

**PCI-DSS** — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard. → [Regulated Environments](12-special-contexts/regulated-environments.md)

**Persona** — A composite representation of a user segment based on research. → [Personas & JTBD](04-product-discovery/personas-and-jtbd.md)

**Platform** — A product designed to be built on top of; its value partly comes from the ecosystem it enables. → [Platform Products](12-special-contexts/platform-products.md)

**PLG (Product-Led Growth)** — A go-to-market strategy where the product is the primary acquisition channel. → [Working with Sales](05-go-to-market/working-with-sales.md)

**PMM (Product Marketing Manager)** — The role that owns product positioning and go-to-market. → [Working with Marketing](05-go-to-market/working-with-marketing.md)

**Positioning** — The strategic choice of where to compete and why you win there. → [Positioning & Differentiation](03-product-strategy/positioning-and-differentiation.md)

**POC (Proof of Concept)** — A limited enterprise engagement to test product fit. → [B2B Products](12-special-contexts/b2b-products.md)

**Porter's Five Forces** — A framework for analyzing competitive dynamics of an industry. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**PRD (Product Requirements Document)** — The primary artifact documenting what to build and why. → [Writing PRDs](02-core-skills/writing-prds.md)

**RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)** — Permission system where access depends on the user's role. → [Regulated Environments](12-special-contexts/regulated-environments.md)

**RICE** — Prioritization framework: Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort. → [Prioritization Frameworks](02-core-skills/prioritization-frameworks.md)

**Rollout** — Staged deployment of a feature, typically from internal → beta → percentage → 100%. → [Launch Planning](05-go-to-market/launch-planning.md)

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)** — The portion of TAM that can be served with the current business model. → [Market Analysis](03-product-strategy/market-analysis.md)

**SOC 2** — Security audit standard validating trust service criteria. → [Regulated Environments](12-special-contexts/regulated-environments.md)

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** — The realistic near-term capture of the SAM. → [Market Analysis](03-product-strategy/market-analysis.md)

**STAR Format** — Interview storytelling structure: Situation, Task, Action, Result. → [Interview Prep](08-career/interview-prep.md)

**Strategy Canvas** — A Blue Ocean tool mapping competitive factors visually. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**Sunk Cost Fallacy** — Continuing investment in a failing direction because of past investment. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — The entire market opportunity at 100% share. → [Market Analysis](03-product-strategy/market-analysis.md)

**Three Horizons Model** — McKinsey framework for managing innovation across time: core, emerging, transformational. → [Strategy Frameworks](07-business-skills/strategy-frameworks.md)

**Usage-Based Pricing** — Charging per unit of consumption rather than per seat. → [Pricing & Packaging](05-go-to-market/pricing-and-packaging.md)

**Value-Based Pricing** — Setting price based on the value delivered to the buyer. → [Pricing & Packaging](05-go-to-market/pricing-and-packaging.md)

**Vanity Metric** — A metric that looks good but doesn't predict business outcomes. → [Data-Driven Decisions](02-core-skills/data-driven-decisions.md)

**Voice of Market** — Qualitative and quantitative input from customers and prospects about their needs and perceptions. → [Market Analysis](03-product-strategy/market-analysis.md)

**Win/Loss Analysis** — Structured analysis of why deals are won or lost against competitors. → [Competitive Intelligence](03-product-strategy/competitive-intelligence.md)

**Wizard of Oz** — An experiment where users think they're using an automated product but humans are performing the function manually. → [MVPs & Experiments](04-product-discovery/mvp-and-experiments.md)

**WSJF (Weighted Shortest Job First)** — Prioritization framework weighting cost of delay against job duration. → [Prioritization Frameworks](02-core-skills/prioritization-frameworks.md)
