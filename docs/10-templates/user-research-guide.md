## Interview Guide

*Use this as a guide, not a script. Follow the participant's thread when interesting.*

### Opening (5 min)

> "Thanks for making time. I want to understand your experience with [topic area] — there's no right or wrong answer and the most valuable feedback is the honest kind. I'm not going to pitch you anything today.
>
> Do you mind if I take notes?"

### Context Setting (10 min)

- Tell me about your role and how [problem area] fits into your day-to-day work.
- What tools do you use for [relevant workflow]?
- Walk me through a typical week — how often does [problem] come up?

### Current Behavior (15 min)

- Can you walk me through the last time you had to [do the thing you're researching]?
- What triggered it? What did you do first?
- Where did things get difficult or take longer than you'd like?
- What did you end up doing as a workaround?

### Problem Depth (10 min)

- [Follow-up on anything that surfaced in current behavior section]
- How important is it for you to solve this problem? What happens if it doesn't get better?
- Have you tried anything else to solve this?

### Alternatives & Comparison (5 min)

- What tools or approaches have you used to try to solve this?
- What did you like about those? What was missing?
- If you could wave a magic wand, what would this look like?

### Closing (5 min)

> "Is there anything about this problem that you think is important that I didn't ask about?"

Thank them. Ask if it's OK to follow up with questions.
